COVID-19 Restrictions has made NY Fashion Week More Accessible Than Ever Before

New York Fashion Week Website. Credit: Aliyah Walker

Access to New York Fashion Week has been coveted among fashion admirers. The event is typically only accessible to those already in the fashion industry or by invitation only. Tickets are also sold for some presentations in sparing quantities; however they are hard to obtain. Due to the restrictions placed on New York Fashion Week this year by Governor Andrew Cuomo, the event became more accessible to the public.

There are typically around 110,000 attendees over the course of New York Fashion Week. In comparsion around 41,200 guests tuned in live just to see Christian Siriano's debut of his Spring 2021 collection.

Many people thought that New York Fashion week was going to be cancelled since the city was a hotspot for COVID-19. But Governor Cuomo annouced that fashion week would take place from September 13th-17th with restrictions.

All of the events had to be in compliance with New York State health and safety guidelines. Due to these regulations, fashion week featured a combination of livestreamed and pre-recorded events.

Normally fashion shows are exclusive to those in the industry. However, as a result of COVID-19, they have become more democratic in a sense. This year the public had access to the shows at the same time as the industry professionals.

In partnership with Lowes Hardware Stores, Siriano debuted his newest collection to the public through a livestream on twitter. At its peak, there were around 41,200 people watching live.
